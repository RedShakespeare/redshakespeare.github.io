Appendix U – ADOM 1.1.1 Monsters – complete list

This is complete monster dump from ADOM 1.1.1 executable. It includes all monsters stats. You need very wide display for good viewing. Alternatively press Ctrl-Minus several times.

Legend

  • ID, Symbol, Name: Identification.
  • Alignment: C-Chaotic, N-Neutral, L-Lawful
  • Size: T-Tiny, S-Small, M-Medium, L-Large, H-Huge. Monster size determines the weight of monster's corpse – tiny-30s, small-60s, medium-120s, large-240s, huge-480s.
  • Category: A-animal, c-cat, i-insect, q-aquatic, C-Construct, e-elemental, &-Demon, D-Dragon, G-Giant, H-Humanoid, J-Jelly, P-Plant, U-Undead. All monsters belong to 0-2 categories. See also 0.9.4.
  • DV/PV, Attack, Speed, HP: Common stats.
  • Special abilities: Things like spells, special attacks, special abilities, etc. See 0.9.15.
  • Immune: Monster's immunities – A-acid, C-cold, F-fire, E-lightning (elec), M-Mindcraft, D-death rays, P-poison, R-petrification, =-water, ^-traps, +-doors, |-webs, *-fear.
  • Vulnerable: Monster's vulnerabilities (they generally take double damage) – C-cold, F-fire, E-lightning. No monsters are vulnerable to acid or anything else.
  • Corpse: Probability that a monster will drop corpse when killed. Can be improved with the "of hunting" suffix.
  • Corpse Effects: What will happen to the PC when monster's corpse is eaten. See also 0.3.3.
  • Darkness? Invisibility? Diagonal?: Whether or not the monster can see in darkness, can see through invisibility, or prefers diagonal movement when pursuing the PC.
  • Wishable?: This is opposite of "Unique?". Some monsters are both unique and wishable though (eg. raider lord, cute dog).
  • Intelect: This governs how inteligently the monster behaves on the battlefield. Influences when and if it starts panicking/berserking, whether or not to pick up and use items, etc. Basically there are 6 levels of intelect: 0/25/55/75/90/100.
  • Tracking: Probability of monster chasing the PC when out of LOS.
  • SR: Spell Resistance. It governs how often a particular monster ignores the effects of certain spells, wands, and Mindcraft. The percent chance for a monster with spell resistance to ignore an effect is reduced by one point for every two of the PC's experience levels; otherwise, the chance for each type of monster is fixed.
  • DL: Danger Level. Monster are not normally generated on dungeon levels with lower DL.
  • Rarity: Relative probability of a monster being generated. Rarity 50 monster is 5 times more likely to be generated than rarity 10 monster, if they are the same DL. Monsters with 0 Rarity are never generated randomly.
